Curried Chicken Pie*

*This recipe was derived from the book Pocket Chef: Pies

Serves: 4

Preparation time: 20 minutes

Cooking time: 40-45 minutes

This chicken pie has many of the ingredients that are used in the Indian cuisine, like garlic, cream and Madras curry powder. It has a yellow gravy and a warm puff pastry.

Any pastry can be used for this pie, along with the other recipes in this book, but it is recommended to make a puff pastry. Click this link if you want to see other types of pastries.

Puff Pastry

Ingredients

  • 1½ cups plain flour
  • Pinch of salt
  • 1 cup unsalted butter
  • ½ cup iced water
  • 1 tsp lemon juice

Method

  1. Sift the flour and salt into a large mixing bowl.
  2. Add a ¼ of the butter and rub into the flour until soft, then gradually add the water and the lemon juice, mixing until a soft, elastic dough forms.
  3. Roll out the dough on a floured surface into a rectangle three times as long as it is wide; it should be approximately 1 cm thick.
  4. Arrange a ¼ of the diced butter across the top ⅔ of the dough. Fold the bottom third up and the top third down, turn 90º to the right and seal the edges
  5. Repeat the steps until all the butter has been used up, turning the pastry 90º each time.
  6. Cover and chill before using.

Pie Filling

Ingredients

  • 2 tbsp unsalted butter
  • 1 large leek, sliced
  • 2 cloves of garlic
  • Salt and freshly ground pepper
  • 1 tbsp Madras curry powder
  • 4 small skinless chicken breasts, diced
  • 1½ cups chicken stock
  • 1½ cups heavy cream
  • Small handful of flat-leaf parsley, finely chopped

Method

  1. Preheat the oven to 180ºC (160ºC fan). Melt the butter in a large saucepan set over meadium heat.
  2. Add the leek, garlic and a little salt and sweat for 6-7 minutes, stirring occasionally, until softened.
  3. Add the curry powder. Stir well, and cook for 1 minute, then add the chicken.
  4. Cover with the chicken stock and bring to a simmer. Cook for 5 minutes, then stir through the cream, parsley and some seasoning.

Pie Preparation

Ingredients

  • Chicken filling
  • Puff pastry
  • Plain flour, for dusting
  • 1 small egg, beaten

Method

  1. Spoon into a large cast-iron pan or baking dish. Roll out the pastry on a lightly floured surface into a round approximately 1cm thick.
  2. Drape over the filling and seal against the inside edge of the pan or dish. Cut a way any excess and bore a small hole in the middle.
  3. Brush with a beaten egg and bake for 20-25 minutes until puffed and goden, then serve.
